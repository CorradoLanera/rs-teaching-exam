Greetings!!!

We agree our final plot is not lovely, nor insightful… but our excuse for that is: it was only a pretext plot ;-)

You are now a master of text and labels into {ggplot2}. Remember to distinguish when your “text” requirements come from:

  • data: map the corresponding variable to the label aesthetic, and draw them with geom_text() or geom_label() at your convenience

  • meta-data: maybe that means you want to manage some title or text in some legends (or axis, which are nothing more than the axes’ legend). So, you can go with the corresponding scale__()function (which the first argument is the legend title), and you can use thebreak, andlabelto control which label to plot and their corresponding name respectively. Or, just to change the title, you can use the convenient wrapperlabs()` just assigning the title you like to the corresponding aesthetics (eg, labs(colour = “Colour legend”)).

  • just an annotation: so, you simply want to draw a geom_* for a single-new not-in-data point ;-)… use the annotation() function,
    and in its first argument, set the geom you like (eg, “text” or “label”), next directly assign the value you need (eg, x, y,
    label).

  • style: maybe you already have some text you like to make more stylish. So, you need to put a call to theme() and adjust the corresponding elements’ characteristics. For text elements they are set calling element_text() for the corresponding argument of interest (eg, plot.title).
